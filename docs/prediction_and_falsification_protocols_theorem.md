# Prediction And Falsification Protocols Theorem

## Target

Construct a finite, network-native prediction-and-falsification protocol
interface downstream of the closed Paper 14 discriminating benchmarks theorem.

The target is a conditional theorem only. It should eventually specify how
prediction targets, falsification thresholds, rejection conditions, benchmark
compatibility, and audit statuses can be stated as finite protocol rows
without silently turning protocol definition into benchmark success, prediction
success, falsification success, physical validation, or unified-field
promotion.

## Closed Rungs

- `PFP-001`: Upstream binding and claim-boundary scaffold through the frozen
  Paper 14 final conditional certificate.
- `PFP-002`: Define finite prediction-and-falsification protocol records.

Closed boundary for `PFP-002`:

- finite protocol identifiers
- finite prediction target labels
- finite observable labels
- finite regime labels
- finite threshold labels
- finite rejection-condition labels
- finite audit-status descriptors
- no protocol recovery claim
- no benchmark success claim
- no prediction success claim
- no falsification success claim
- no physical promotion
- no physical validation
- no empirical adequacy claim
- no observed-catalog recovery claim
- no simulation-only or fit-only shortcut
- no unified field theory claim

## Active Rung

## Planned Rungs

## Residual Risk

The main risk is category error: confusing a protocol-definition interface
with evidence that a prediction succeeded or an alternative was falsified.
Guards must keep protocol construction separate from benchmark success,
prediction success, falsification success, physical validation, and physical
promotion.
